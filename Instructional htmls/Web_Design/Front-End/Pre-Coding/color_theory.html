Terminology

Primary Colors

  • Depending who is talking about this, primary colors can either be the three primary colors which are the most basic: Blue, Red, and Yellow; or the base color one is using for one's project. The textbook definition is the three most basic color though.

Secondary Colors

  • Also depends on what someone is talking about; but secondary colors are supposed to mean the combination of primary colors in equal amount. So when you mix blue and red you get purple, red and yellow you get orange, and yellow and blue creates green. Those are what are known as secondary colors in the textbook.
  • However, the term is also loosely used to refer to the secondary color in a color scheme used in a website. So forexample one has used red as his main color and yellow as a secondary color in their website, yellow becomes the secondary color.

Tertiary Colors

  • These are obtained by mixing primary colors with secondary colors; so if you mix blue with purple you get blue violet for example, blue with green you get cyan, etc.
  • They are sometimes called intermediary colors.

Hue

  • This is the color itself.
  • It doesn't matter if it is primary/secondary/tertiary. As long as it is in its full saturation, hue refers to any color.

Tone

  • Refers to the degree of brightness in the color.

Shade

  • Refers to the degree of blackness in the color.

Saturation and Brigthness

  • Low Saturation / Brightness: Conveys soft emotions, absence of danger
  • Low Brightness: Conveys luxury, wealth, and elegance, which lean well with feelings of passiveness/contentment created by low saturated colors.
  • High Saturation / Brightness Conveys strong emotions such as boldness and confidence etc. They're used to grab attention and create a memorable impression.

Color Harmony and Selection Tools

Color Rules

  • These are general schemes used in the creation of color palettes. They are as following:
    • Analogous: Picks two colors in the similar vicinity to the base color chosen.
    • Monochromatics: Tints and Shades
    • Triad:
    • Complimentary: Most contrasting color to the base color
    • Split complimentary: Gives you two options of the contrasting color from the complimentary scheme.
    • Double Split complimentary: Gives you two more options from the base color side of the complimentary color scheme
    • Shades Just gives you different shades of the base color.
  • You can find these palette creation modes in most of the palette creators mentioned below.

Color Scheme Generators

  • These are tools you can use to create color palette for your project.
  • They are many. Most of them are mentioned below.
    • Adobe Color Wheel: This is one of the most popular. You can find it here
    • Color Space: Also a very good and handy tool which has a lot of options. You can find it here
    • Color Hunt
    • Muzli Color: Great for finding different palettes from a single color. Find it here.
    • Web Gradients: Good for gradients, especially as backgrounds to webpages. You go to the website, choose the gradient you want, and the site will give you that gradient in css code. The site is here.
    • Dribble: This gives you shows you different pictures which have items of different colors. When you want the color of any item therein, you just hover above the color and it shows you the color code.
