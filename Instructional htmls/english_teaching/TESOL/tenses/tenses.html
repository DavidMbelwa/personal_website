You should be familiar with English tenses by now. If you aren't refer to the table below

Simple Continous Perfect Perfect Continous
Past I played. I was playing. I had played. I had been playing.
Present I play. I am playing. I have played. I have been playing.
Future I will play. I will be playing. I will have played. I will have been playing.

Simple Past

  • It talks about events that happened at a certain point in the past.
  • It just applies the past form of the main verb. Eg. He danced the whole night.
  • It uses "did" for questions and negative forms.

    • - Did he dance the whole night?

    - Yes, he did. (Or "Yes, he did dance the whole night" / "Yes, he danced the whole night")

    - No, he didn't. (Or, "No, he didn't dance the whole night.")

Past Continous

  • Talks about events of the past that were happening at that time.
  • It uses auxillary verbs was/were followed by "ing" at the end of the main verb.

    • I was walking when I saw Mariah Carrie down the street.

    He was cooking when his wife called him.

  • The question and negative forms also uses the same auxillary verbs, either at the beginning or after the subject of the sentence.

    • - Were they talking when the boss entered?

    - Yes, they were.

    - No, they weren't.

Simple Present

  • Talks about regular events, habits, or states of things.
  • Uses the original form of the verb for the first and second person personal pronouns (i.e. I, You, We) and plural third person pronoun (i.e. they). For the third person singular personal pronouns (i.e. He, She, It) it adds "s" or "es" at the end of the verb. Refer this list in order to know which verbs take an "
