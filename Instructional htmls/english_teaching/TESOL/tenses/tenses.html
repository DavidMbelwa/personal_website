You should be familiar with English tenses by now. If you aren't refer to the table below

Simple Continous Perfect Perfect Continous
Past I played. I was playing. I had played. I had been playing.
Present I play. I am playing. I have played. I have been playing.
Future I will play. I will be playing. I will have played. I will have been playing.

Simple Past:

Talks about events that happened at a certain point in the past.

  • It just applies the past form of the main verb. Eg. He danced the whole night.
  • It uses "did" for questions and negative forms.

    • - Did he dance the whole night?

    - Yes, he did. (Or "Yes, he did dance the whole night" / "Yes, he danced the whole night")

    - No, he didn't. (Or, "No, he didn't dance the whole night.")
